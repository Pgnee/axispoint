Dr. Edgar Underwood brings more than four decades of experience in vascular and diagnostic imaging to Grandview Medical Center. A tenured professor at the University of Alabama at Birmingham, he continues to contribute his expertise on a courtesy basis, mentoring the next generation of interventional radiologists while maintaining a practice centered on advanced venous care.

Education

  • B.A. in Biology, Southwestern College at Memphis (now Rhodes College), 1972
  • M.D., Emory University, 1976
  • Residency in Diagnostic Radiology, Baptist Medical Center (1988–1992)
  • Fellowship in Interventional Radiology, University of Alabama at Birmingham (1992–1993)

Board Certifications

  • Board Certified in Radiology, American Board of Radiology (1995)
  • Licensed to practice in Alabama since 1978

Professional Experience

  • Professor of Radiology, University of Alabama at Birmingham (2012–present)
  • Chief of Interventional Radiology, Princeton Baptist Medical Center (1993–2006)
  • Emergency medicine and interventional radiology roles across several Alabama hospitals, including UAB and the VA Hospital
  • Recognized with the International Society of Endovascular Specialists Milestone Award (2009) and repeated "Best Doctors in America" honors (2011, 2013, 2014)

Clinical Interests

Dr. Underwood's research has focused on percutaneous tumor ablation and chronic cerebrospinal venous insufficiency, and he has been an active participant in clinical trials evaluating vascular stents, embolization techniques, and treatments for hepatocellular cancer.

His long clinical career spans the full range of venous and arterial disease management, including varicose vein treatment and embolization-based therapies for both benign and malignant vascular conditions, including uterine fibroid embolization.
